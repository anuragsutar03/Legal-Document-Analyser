TITLE: Traffic and Motor Vehicle Law Basics

SECTION 1: Driving Licence Requirements
Driving a motor vehicle on a public road generally requires a valid driving licence appropriate to the class of vehicle being driven. Driving without a valid licence, or allowing an unlicensed person to drive one's vehicle, is typically a punishable offence attracting fines and, in serious or repeated cases, further legal consequences.

SECTION 2: Vehicle Documents
A driver is typically required to carry and produce on demand a valid driving licence, the vehicle's registration certificate, a valid insurance policy (usually at least third-party insurance), and a pollution-under-control certificate where applicable. Driving without valid insurance is generally an offence, since third-party insurance is usually mandatory.

SECTION 3: Traffic Violations and Penalties
Common traffic violations include speeding, jumping a red light, not wearing a seatbelt or helmet, using a mobile phone while driving, and driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs. Penalties typically range from fines to licence suspension, and repeated or serious violations, such as drunk driving, can carry criminal consequences including imprisonment.

SECTION 4: Motor Accident Compensation
A person injured, or the legal heirs of a person killed, in a motor vehicle accident may be entitled to compensation through a Motor Accident Claims process, generally regardless of who was at fault under certain no-fault provisions, with fuller compensation typically available upon establishing fault or negligence of the other party.

SECTION 5: Hit and Run Cases
In cases where the vehicle or driver responsible for an accident cannot be traced, victims or their families may still be entitled to a fixed compensation amount under hit-and-run provisions, typically through a government-administered compensation scheme funded for this purpose.

SECTION 6: Duties After an Accident
A driver involved in an accident causing injury is generally expected to take reasonable steps to assist the injured person, such as arranging medical aid, and to report the accident to the police within a specified period, rather than fleeing the scene, which can itself carry additional legal consequences.
