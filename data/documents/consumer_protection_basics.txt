TITLE: Consumer Protection Basics

SECTION 1: Who Is a Consumer
A consumer is any person who buys goods or hires services for consideration (payment), including part payment or deferred payment, for personal use. A person who buys goods for resale or for a commercial purpose is generally not considered a consumer, except when the goods are used solely for earning a livelihood through self-employment.

SECTION 2: Right to Safety
Consumers have the right to be protected against goods and services that are hazardous to life and property. Manufacturers and sellers are expected to meet safety standards, and defective or dangerous products can give rise to liability claims.

SECTION 3: Right to Information
Consumers have the right to accurate information about the quality, quantity, purity, standard, and price of goods or services, so they can make informed choices and protect themselves against unfair or misleading trade practices.

SECTION 4: Deficiency in Service and Unfair Trade Practices
A deficiency in service refers to any fault, imperfection, shortcoming, or inadequacy in the quality, nature, or manner of performance that a service provider was required to maintain. An unfair trade practice includes false representation of goods or services, misleading advertisements, or deceptive pricing intended to promote sale in a way that harms the consumer.

SECTION 5: Where to File a Complaint
Consumer complaints regarding defective goods, deficient services, overcharging, or unfair trade practices can typically be filed before a Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission, with jurisdiction depending on the value of goods or services and compensation claimed. Complaints can generally be filed without needing a lawyer, and remedies may include replacement, refund, compensation, or removal of the defect.

SECTION 6: Time Limit to File
A consumer complaint generally must be filed within two years from the date on which the cause of action arose, such as the date of purchase or the date the deficiency in service was discovered, though delays may be condoned by the Commission for sufficient cause.
