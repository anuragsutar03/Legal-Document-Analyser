TITLE: Employment and Workplace Basics

SECTION 1: The Employment Contract / Offer Letter
An employment relationship is generally governed by the offer letter or employment contract, which sets out salary, designation, notice period, probation terms, and grounds for termination. Even where no formal contract exists, an employment relationship can still be established through consistent conduct, such as regular payment of wages and control over work.

SECTION 2: Notice Period and Termination
A notice period is the time either the employer or employee must give before ending the employment relationship, as specified in the contract. Termination without following the agreed notice period or without valid cause may entitle the affected party to compensation in lieu of notice, though specific rules vary depending on the nature of employment and applicable labour law.

SECTION 3: Wrongful Termination
Termination that is arbitrary, discriminatory, or made without following the procedure specified in the employment contract or applicable law (such as failing to provide notice, a valid reason, or an opportunity to respond to allegations) may be challenged as wrongful termination, potentially entitling the employee to reinstatement or compensation.

SECTION 4: Unpaid Wages and Salary Delays
Employees have a right to timely payment of wages for work performed. Persistent delay or non-payment of salary can be pursued through a written demand, a complaint to the appropriate labour authority, or in some cases through civil proceedings for recovery of dues.

SECTION 5: Workplace Harassment
Employees have a right to a safe workplace free from harassment, including sexual harassment. Organizations above a certain size are typically required to have an Internal Complaints Committee to investigate harassment complaints, and employees are entitled to file a complaint without fear of retaliation.

SECTION 6: Provident Fund and Statutory Benefits
Employees in eligible organizations are often entitled to statutory benefits such as provident fund contributions, gratuity after a minimum period of continuous service, and other welfare benefits, which employers are legally required to provide and cannot contract out of.
